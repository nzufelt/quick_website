Systems and Norms for the Workshop

Communication is on Slack

We would like to keep our communication out of our email inboxes and in a consistent and structured place. Slack excels at allowing organizations to communicate and generate ideas both synchronously (acting a bit like an instant message system) and asynchronously (acting more like email). While Slack may be used to share files, it typically does not excel at being a file repository, and channels that are used primarily to share files tend to be less useful, eventually becoming a virtual dumping ground of lost ideas. Thus, when we say that Slack is our communication tool, this means multiple things:

  • It is our communication tool: You are expected to be responsive on Slack, as that will be the main way in which we communicate with each other.
  • It is our only communication tool: We typically will not use email, and we encourage students to keep their Workshop-related communication out of other messaging systems such as (Facebook) Messenger.
  • It is only our communication tool: We will not use it to submit ‚Äúassignments‚Äù or regularly share files, or for any purpose other than communication. Our primary tool for file sharing will be Google Drive (see below). There may be exceptions to this rule, but they should be exceptional.

Slack protocols

The keys to success with a tool like Slack tend to rely around following certain rules, or protocols.

  • Check for Location: Pay attention to the channel in which you‚Äôre posting. Does it make sense to post your message there? Is it something that all (or most) of those people need to know?   • Share with Links: Slack has good support for hyperlinking. Advising happens with people

One of the key elements which makes the Workshop special is a breakdown of the traditional hierarchy of disciplinary/departmental focuses. However, it is still helpful for us to have a smaller number of adult-student pairings so that students know whom to contact for organizational or logistical questions or with whom to check their work.

We will assemble into a collection of advisory groups within the Workshop team with 2 faculty advisors and 4-6 students whom they are advising. See the document /Organizational/Student Advisory Pairings for the actual pairings. There is a top-level folder in Google Drive for advisories so that you can share documents in a smaller group setting as needed.
